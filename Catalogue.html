Book Title: Srimad Bhagavad Gita Yatharoop

Author: Maharishi Veda Vyasa


Price: $100.00
The Bhagavad Gita is set in the form of a dialogue between Prince Arjuna and the god Krishna, who serves as his charioteer. This dialogue takes place on the battlefield just before the Kurukshetra War, where Arjuna is filled with moral dilemma and despair about fighting in the battle. Arjuna is confused and morally troubled about fighting in the war against his own relatives, friends, and teachers. In response to Arjuna's confusion, Krishna imparts spiritual wisdom and guidance to Arjuna. He discusses various paths to spiritual realization, the nature of reality, the self, duty (dharma), righteousness, and the paths to liberation (moksha). The Gita addresses the concepts of duty, righteousness, and devotion, and it explores the moral and ethical dilemmas faced by individuals. It teaches the paths of devotion, action, and knowledge, emphasizing the importance of selfless action and devotion to God.
